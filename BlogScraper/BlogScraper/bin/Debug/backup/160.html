iPod Shuffle + iTunes, I'm Hooked!

iPod Shuffle + iTunes, I'm Hooked!

noahc
25 Jun 2006 5:09 AM
  • Comments 15

During TechEd 2006, my buddy Jean-Luc David (JL), let me borrow his iPod Shuffle.  Oh my gosh!!  This little feller is awesome!!  I just love the pure simplicity, elegance, easy of use, and… style.  So beautiful!  Of course, I had to use iTunes to put music on it and I fell in love with another Apple product.  I’ve probobly spent over a dozen hours the last week updating meta data and CD cover art in iTunes.

My whole philosophy on technology and computers is that they're here for convenience.  Convenience of time & communications.  Enjoyment is in there too for those gamers, or weirdos that would rather program for fun (aka, me).  So I like simplicity in technology and computers, but extensibility to let me make it do just what I want to.  I’m a gadget guy, but only if really useful.  For example, I used to use PDAs, but just stick with a pysical paper note pad and pen now (which gets looks in meetings at MS) for notetaking.  Apple just seams to strike an amazing balance of elegance, easy of use, and practicality.

So at the end of TechEd, JL actually GAVE me his 512MB Shuffle!!!  Wow, that is continuously one generous guy.  Upon getting home and showing it off to my wife, she got into it and I give it to her so she could listen to Podcasts of the Dave Ramsey Show (on finances).  My next decision, do I get a Nano, iPod, or another Shuffle?  Well, being on a budget and after four trips to the Apple Store, I decided to go with my own Shuffle for now.  Later I may get a Nano or iPod (birthday? Christmas? ), but for now, this is perfect.

There are quite a number of iPod users at MS.  You’ll see them walking around campus everyday with those distinctive headphones.  Of course it’s not encouraged, but not prohibited either.  You just can’t stop people from using incredible products.  I still feel a little apprehension about showing it off publicly at work, but it is so much fun to wear it around town.  It feels like “yeah, I’m part of the cool crowd now”… that’s a powerful feeling for marketing.

My last player was a 6GB Rio Carbon Pearl. You know, I rarely used it for anything than playing all songs on random order.  It has a good capacity, looks great, and the best part is that it mounts as a Mass Storage Device (MSD).  However, each time it is disconnected, it takes a while (several minutes) to re-parse all the files and meta data.  Of course, I’d use Windows Media Player.  WMP is okay, and keeps getting better, but dang, iTunes is so simple and elegant.

I tried a bunch of headphones and ear buds with my Rio Carbon (including the Bose TriPort & Sony Fontopia), but the Rio didn’t have the output to drive them, especially on an airplane or in a noisy area.  The first thing I noticed with the Shuffle was it has a much higher amplifier capability.  It drives those Sony’s so well they sound just incredible!  I haven’t hit the limit of the Shuffle’s amp yet.  I can hardly believe I got by with the lousy amp of the Rio!

Love It List!
Damn it’s good stuff!

  • Shuffle: Slick, simple!!  Just plugs right into the USB port, no additional cables needed.  Perfect.
  • Shuffle: It’s got a 9–pin USB socket!  This is sooo coool!  The 4 normal USB pins, plus 5 iPod control pins.  Being a USB device electronic gizmo inventor myself, this is just way too cool.
  • Shuffle: The USB hardware has a click-in connector.  This is really cool for caps, lanyards, etc.  USB memory keys should do this too for lanyards.  Leave it up to Apple to design great hardware.
  • iTunes: Easy COM API (of course, top item is programmability)
  • iTunes: Great Meta Tag Editor (bulk editing is a breeze)
  • iTunes: Super easy and fast to use (holy cow this is easy!)
  • iTunes: Incredible searching capabilities (fast and damn easy)
  • iTunes: Rip CD into 160k MP3 (by default), just pop a disc in

Suggestions
Boy I wish it…

  • Updated meta data in the background (instead of in a model progress dialog)!!

Bugs
Unfortunately, all software has bugs.  For some reason I seam to come across them fast and frequently in Mac software. But they’re always minor and can be worked around easily.

  • Context menu keyboard key doesn't work on song listings
  • Shift-Arrow selection is not consistent with other PC apps (up & down are odd)
  • If you edit meta data on tracks not yet ripped, while ripping CDs (ex: add album art), the changes are lost
  • Sound is written directly to the sound card instead of the Windows OS, so it won’t play through Remote Desktop.

Questions
Stuff I don’t know how to do yet in iTunes (if it’s supported at all, don’t think so, but before I say it isn’t, they’re questions)

  • How do you get iTunes to reload ID3 tag info?  Scenario: I edited meta data externally, now I want iTunes to pick up the changes.
  • How to remove tracks from iTunes that have been deleted from disc?

AND THEN, Accessories!!

Oh boy, this is the perfect marketing plan for gizmo geeks!!  There’s just soooo much out there.  I got a dedicated catalog of iPod accessories from JRMusic.com, and it was some of my favorite toilet-side reading for a while.  Unfortunately there isn’t nearly as much stuff out there for the Shuffle as there is for the other iPods, but here’s what’s on my wish list (and I just purchased a few tonight).  They can get pricy fast… but they’re just so cool!

In conclusion…
Of course, I hope MS creates the most awesome hand-held media device ever and keeps up some competition, which always drives better products.  But MS’ focus is on long-term all-in-one devices that combine the cell phone, music, media, etc.  According to BillG, he believes iPods won’t survive in the long term, but you sure as heck can’t deny their incredible popularity and penetration today.  When cell phones get easier to use (instead of more complex) w/ significant storage, I’ll be a believer of the all-in-one as well.  In the mean time, I’m loving the simplicity and elegance of the iPod Shuffle.
